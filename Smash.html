The world of Esports is vast, and while a lot of fans may enjoy many of the different games such as Street Fighter or Overwatch, many also commit to playing just one competitively. My game is Super Smash Bros. There are many different games in the franchise like Melee and Ultimate, each with their own unique player and fanbases. I personally have long been a part of 3 of these communities being Melee, Project M, and Ultimate. There are many different tournaments that are run of different sizes, the most common 2 are majors like Genesis, and locals that are generally run weekly or bi-weekly. The biggest event for Smash Bros. and almost every other fighting game Esport has always been Evo, a yearly event held that hosts almost all relevant fighting games of the time. Unfortunately Smash has been dropped from its roster as of 2022. 

If the world of Esports interests you, my single biggest piece fo advice is honestly to make sure you stretch your hands. It may seem silly, but when you spend long hours at a time practicing to get better at specific skills in game you can seriously injure your hands if you aren't careful.
